Exchange Archive
Artists do not create work in a vacuum.*

All artists work in dialog with other people. Why isn’t this dialog visible? In all other fields, footnotes are the norm. Exchange Archive hopes to recognize and support artistic dialog and research by acknowledging the references, influences, and inspirations that flow between projects.

Please add your work to the archive, as well as the projects that influenced this work. If you are not an artist, feel free to suggest possible connections, inspirations, or potential dialog between art works.

Thank you!
MultiAgency Collective, Caroline Woolard, MoMA’s Education Department

*Vacuums are for cleaning, not for divine inspiration. Andy Warhol traded a print for a vacuum from Sydney Lewis. He also put vacuums in plexi and called it art. He made work in a Factory with 100 other artists.
